What's Covered

25+ Covered Conditions

Most critical illness policies cover a core list of serious conditions. The 3 most common — cancer, heart attack, and stroke — account for over 75% of all CI claims in Canada. Here are the most frequently covered:

🎗️

Cancer

Life-threatening malignant tumours, including leukemia, lymphoma, and most forms of cancer diagnosed by a specialist. Early-stage cancers may have partial benefits.

Most common CI claim in Canada
❤️

Heart Attack

Acute myocardial infarction with permanent damage to heart muscle, confirmed by specific ECG changes and elevated cardiac enzymes consistent with a diagnosis.

2nd most common CI claim
🧠

Stroke

Cerebrovascular accident resulting in permanent neurological deficit lasting more than 30 days. Covered when caused by thrombosis, hemorrhage, or embolism.

3rd most common CI claim
🫀

Coronary Artery Bypass

Open heart surgery to correct narrowing or blockage of coronary arteries through bypass grafting. One of the most common cardiac surgeries in Canada.

Covered on most CI plans
🧬

Multiple Sclerosis

Confirmed diagnosis of multiple sclerosis with specific neurological symptoms persisting for a defined period. Progressive and relapsing forms typically covered.

Covered on most CI plans
🫁

Major Organ Transplant

Transplant of heart, kidney, liver, lung, or pancreas, or bone marrow transplant. Coverage begins when the insured is placed on the transplant waiting list.

Covered on most CI plans
🦽

Paralysis

Complete and permanent loss of use of two or more limbs due to injury or disease, including paraplegia and quadriplegia. Must be total and irreversible.

Covered on comprehensive plans
👁️

Blindness

Total and irreversible loss of vision in both eyes. Must be permanent and not correctable by surgery, medication, or corrective lenses.

Covered on comprehensive plans
➕

+18 More Conditions

Including Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, ALS, kidney failure, aortic surgery, heart valve replacement, loss of speech, deafness, severe burns, and more.

Varies by insurer & plan
⭐ Featured Option

Return of Premium —
You Can't Lose

Return of Premium (ROP) is a unique rider that gives you back 100% of the premiums you paid if you never make a critical illness claim. It's the only insurance product where you win either way — protection if you get sick, money back if you stay healthy.

💰
100% Premiums Returned

If you reach the end of the policy term without claiming — every dollar you paid comes back to you.

🛡️
Full CI Coverage Maintained

You're fully covered throughout the entire policy — ROP doesn't reduce your benefit amount.

📈
Forced Tax-Free Savings

Think of it as a savings plan with free insurance on top. The returned premiums are tax-free.

🔄
On Death Benefit

Most ROP plans also return premiums to your estate if you die without making a CI claim.

How Return of Premium Works

1

You Purchase a CI Policy with ROP

Example: $250,000 coverage, 20-year term, with Return of Premium rider. Monthly premium: ~$150/month.

2

Scenario A — You Make a Claim

Diagnosed with cancer at year 10? You receive $250,000 tax-free within 30 days of diagnosis. Use it however you need.

3

Scenario B — You Never Claim

Reach year 20 healthy? You receive $36,000 back (20 years × $150/mo × 12). Every dollar returned, tax-free.

4

Scenario C — You Pass Away

If you die without claiming, most ROP plans return premiums to your estate — protecting your family either way.
