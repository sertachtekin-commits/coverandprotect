Super Visa Insurance for Parents from India: A Complete 2026 Guide

By Sertac Tekin, Licensed Ontario Insurance Advisor (FSRA #10112782) · Updated July 2026 · 7 min read

The Super Visa lets parents and grandparents from India stay in Canada for up to 5 years per visit — but the application won't be approved without qualifying medical insurance. Here's exactly what's required in 2026, what it costs, and how to get it right the first time.

India is one of the largest sources of Super Visa applications in Canada, and the insurance step is where families most often get stuck. This guide walks through the requirement, real 2026 pricing, the pre-existing condition question that matters most, and how to buy the policy from Canada.

The Super Visa insurance requirement (2026)

To approve a Super Visa, IRCC requires proof of a qualifying medical insurance policy that:

  • Provides at least $100,000 in emergency medical coverage;
  • Is valid for a minimum of 365 days from the date of entry;
  • Covers healthcare, hospitalization, and repatriation;
  • Comes from a Canadian insurer or an IRCC-designated provider; and
  • Is paid in full (or on an approved monthly plan) with proof submitted alongside the application.

Canada's healthcare system does not cover visitors, so this policy is both an immigration requirement and a genuine financial safety net for your parents.

Buy the policy in Canada, not India

A common question from Indian families: "Can we buy the insurance back home?" For Super Visa purposes, the answer is generally no — the policy must be from a Canadian insurer or an IRCC-approved provider. In practice, the Canadian resident child or grandchild buys the policy here on behalf of the visiting parent. You can arrange it before your parent's medical, and adjust the start date to their planned arrival.

The pre-existing conditions question (read this carefully)

Many parents from India manage conditions like type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, or heart disease. Two things matter here:

  • Declare everything honestly. The medical questionnaire is the foundation of the policy. A small omission can void coverage exactly when your family needs it.
  • Choose a plan that covers stable pre-existing conditions. "Stable" usually means no change to medication, dosage, or symptoms for a defined period (often 90 or 180 days) before the coverage start date.
Practical tip: If your parent recently had a medication change, it may be worth aligning the policy's coverage start date so the stability window works in your favour. This is a detail an experienced advisor plans around — it can be the difference between a paid and a denied claim.

Step-by-step: how to arrange it

  • 1. Gather your parent's date of birth and a short summary of any health conditions and medications.
  • 2. Get quotes for $100,000 coverage, 365-day term, with and without pre-existing condition coverage.
  • 3. Choose a deductible that balances premium and out-of-pocket comfort.
  • 4. Purchase the policy (annual or approved monthly) and set the start date to the planned arrival.
  • 5. Submit the proof of insurance with the Super Visa application.

Frequently asked questions

What insurance do parents from India need for a Super Visa?

A qualifying policy with at least $100,000 in emergency medical coverage, valid for a minimum of 365 days from entry, from a Canadian insurer or IRCC-approved provider. Proof is submitted with the application.

Can the insurance be bought in Canada rather than India?

Yes, and it usually should be — the policy must be from a Canadian insurer or an IRCC-designated provider. The Canadian resident child typically buys it on behalf of the visiting parent.

How much does Super Visa insurance cost for Indian parents?

Typically $1,000 to $6,000+ per year for $100,000 coverage, driven mainly by the parent's age and health. Pre-existing condition coverage adds to the premium.

Does Super Visa insurance cover pre-existing conditions like diabetes?

Many plans cover stable pre-existing conditions such as controlled diabetes or blood pressure, subject to a stability period. Declare conditions honestly and choose a plan that covers them to avoid denied claims.
