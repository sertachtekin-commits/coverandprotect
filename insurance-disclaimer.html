Insurance Disclaimer

This page explains important limitations, licensing details, and insurance-product disclosures.

Cover & Protect is an Ontario-licensed independent insurance advisory practice operated by Sertac Tekin. FSRA Licence #10112782.

1. Licensed Advisor Disclosure

Sertac Tekin is licensed in Ontario and regulated by the Financial Services Regulatory Authority of Ontario (FSRA). Licensing can be verified through FSRA's public registry.

2. Independent Advisory Practice

Cover & Protect is an independent advisory practice that may compare products from multiple Canadian insurers and insurance providers. We are not an insurance company and do not underwrite insurance policies.

3. Underwriting and Final Approval

All insurance applications, premiums, coverage amounts, exclusions, limitations, riders, policy conditions, renewals, refunds, cancellations, and claims are subject to the issuing insurer's underwriting rules and policy documents.

4. No Binding Coverage from Website Use

Submitting a website form, speaking with an advisor, receiving an estimate, or reviewing a quote does not create coverage. Coverage begins only when the insurer accepts the application, confirms the effective date, and receives any required payment.

5. Product Availability

Insurance products and eligibility vary by insurer, age, residency, health information, destination, coverage period, occupation, business size, and other underwriting factors. Some products may not be available to all applicants.

6. Premium Estimates

Any premium ranges or examples shown on this website are for general orientation only. Final premiums may be higher or lower and may change based on underwriting, medical history, deductible, coverage amount, travel dates, policy term, and insurer pricing.

7. Claims

Claims are adjudicated by the issuing insurer according to the policy wording. Cover & Protect may assist clients through the claims process, but it does not control insurer claim decisions.

8. Professional Advice

Insurance recommendations should be reviewed in the context of your personal, family, business, tax, and financial circumstances. For tax, legal, or estate matters, consult a qualified professional in that field.
