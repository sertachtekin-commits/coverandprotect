Complete Guide to Super Visa Insurance in Ontario

Super Visa insurance protects parents and grandparents visiting Canada against unexpected emergency medical expenses. It is also a key document for the Super Visa application process.

What Super Visa insurance covers

Typical plans may cover emergency medical treatment, hospitalization, ambulance, emergency prescriptions, repatriation, and certain follow-up services. Exact benefits depend on the insurer and policy wording.

What affects the cost?

Premiums usually depend on age, coverage amount, deductible, medical history, pre-existing condition stability, length of coverage, and insurer pricing. A higher deductible can reduce premium, but it increases out-of-pocket risk.

Pre-existing conditions

Some plans may cover stable pre-existing conditions if the insurer's stability requirements are met. Stability periods and definitions vary. This is one of the most important details to review before buying.

Refunds and cancellations

Refund rules vary by insurer. Some policies may provide a refund if the visa is refused or if the insured person does not travel, provided the insurer's documentation and timing rules are met.

Common mistakes

Common mistakes include buying based only on price, ignoring deductible impact, overlooking stability clauses, missing effective dates, or assuming a quote means coverage is already active.
