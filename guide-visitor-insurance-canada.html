Visitor to Canada Insurance Explained

Visitor insurance helps protect people visiting Canada from large emergency medical bills. It can be useful for tourists, parents visiting family, new immigrants waiting for health coverage, returning Canadians, and temporary residents.

Coverage, premium, exclusions, refunds, and claims are determined by the issuing insurer. A quote is not active coverage until the insurer confirms acceptance and effective date.

Who should consider visitor insurance?

Visitors without provincial health coverage should consider emergency medical insurance before arriving in Canada. Medical treatment can be expensive, and provincial plans usually do not cover visitors.

What can it cover?

Plans may include emergency doctor visits, hospital stays, ambulance, diagnostic tests, emergency prescriptions, repatriation, and accidental dental emergency coverage. Policy wording controls the final coverage.

Cost factors

Cost depends on age, trip length, coverage amount, deductible, medical history, stability of pre-existing conditions, and the selected insurer.

Visitor insurance vs Super Visa insurance

Super Visa insurance is specifically tied to the Super Visa application rules. Visitor insurance is broader and applies to many visitors who do not require Super Visa documentation.

How to compare plans

Compare deductible, coverage amount, exclusions, stability clauses, refund rules, emergency assistance process, and claim procedure rather than premium alone.
