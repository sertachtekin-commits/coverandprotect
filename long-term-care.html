The Reality of Aging in Canada

Care Is Likely — and Costly

We're living longer than ever, and with longer life comes a greater chance of needing help with daily living. Provincial healthcare covers only part of the picture — and waitlists for subsidized care are long. Most of the cost of home care, retirement residences, and long-term care homes is paid out of pocket.

🏠

Home Care

Personal support and nursing at home can run into thousands of dollars per month for regular care.

🏢

Retirement Residence

Private retirement living with care services is a significant monthly cost, rising with the level of care needed.

🏥

Long-Term Care Home

Subsidized spots have long waitlists; private and preferred accommodation carry meaningful out-of-pocket costs.

Care costs vary widely by location, level of care, and accommodation. These are general illustrations, not quotes.

An Honest Note

What Happened to Long-Term Care Insurance?

For years, Canadians could buy a dedicated long-term care insurance policy that paid a benefit when you could no longer perform daily activities on your own. Over the past several years, most major Canadian insurers stopped offering new standalone LTC policies — the product became difficult to price and sustain.

That doesn't mean you can't plan for care costs. It means the smart approach today combines several tools that each do part of the job — so your savings and your family are protected if care is ever needed. As an independent advisor, my role is to help you build that plan honestly, without selling you a product that no longer fits the market.

Your Options Today

How Canadians Plan for Care Costs Now

💪 Critical Illness Insurance

Pays a tax-free lump sum on diagnosis of a covered condition — money you can use for care, home modifications, or to replace income. A cornerstone of modern care planning.

🛡️ Life Insurance with Living Benefits

Certain permanent life policies include riders or advance-access features that can help fund care needs while living, while still protecting your estate.

📋 Disability Coverage

For those still working, disability insurance protects your income if illness or injury prevents you from earning — preserving the savings you'll rely on later.

📈 Dedicated Savings & Investments

Segregated funds, TFSAs, RRSPs and other vehicles can be earmarked for future care, with strategies to protect and grow them over time.

🏠 Home Equity & Estate Planning

Your home and estate plan are part of the picture. Coordinating them with your coverage helps ensure care costs don't erode what you leave behind.

🤝 A Coordinated Plan

No single product solves it. The value of an advisor is fitting these pieces together around your health, budget, and family — and revisiting it as life changes.

When to Start

The Best Time to Plan Is Before You Need It

Coverage like critical illness and life insurance is generally less expensive — and easier to qualify for — when you're younger and healthier. Waiting until a health scare often means higher costs or limited options.

If you're in your 40s, 50s, or early 60s, this is the ideal window to put a care-cost plan in place. If you're helping aging parents think through their options, that conversation matters too — and I'm glad to guide your whole family.
