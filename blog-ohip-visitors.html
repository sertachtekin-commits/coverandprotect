Does OHIP Cover Visitors to Canada? What Families Need to Know (2026)

By Sertac Tekin, Licensed Ontario Insurance Advisor (FSRA #10112782) · Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

Short answer: no. OHIP — the Ontario Health Insurance Plan — covers eligible Ontario residents only. Visitors, tourists, and visiting parents or grandparents (including Super Visa holders) are not covered, and hospitals bill uninsured non-residents directly. One emergency can cost a family thousands of dollars, which is exactly the gap visitor insurance exists to fill.

Every week we speak with families surprised by this — often after a visiting parent has already arrived. Canada's healthcare is "free" for residents, so people assume a guest staying with a resident family is somehow included. They aren't. Here's how it actually works in 2026.

Who OHIP does and doesn't cover

OHIP covers people who make Ontario their primary home and hold a qualifying immigration status — Canadian citizens, permanent residents, and certain work- and study-permit holders, among others. Coverage is tied to residency, not to being physically in Ontario.

That leaves these common situations outside OHIP entirely:

  • Tourists and short-term visitors — no coverage, regardless of how long the trip is.
  • Parents and grandparents visiting family — including multi-month stays and Super Visa visits.
  • Visitors staying with OHIP-covered relatives — a resident's coverage never extends to their guests.
  • People waiting on immigration status that doesn't yet qualify for OHIP.

Good news for newcomers: the waiting period is gone

One rule that changed recently and still confuses people: Ontario used to make even eligible new residents wait three months for OHIP. That waiting period was permanently eliminated in 2024 — newcomers who meet the residency and status requirements are eligible from their first day in Ontario.

But read that carefully: it removed the wait for people who qualify. It did not extend OHIP to visitors or tourists — they don't qualify at all, no matter how long they stay.

What uninsured care actually costs in Ontario

Uninsured non-residents are billed at out-of-country rates, and the numbers add up fast:

These are illustrative ranges — actual charges vary by hospital and treatment — but the direction is clear: a single unexpected event can turn a family visit into a financial crisis.

How visitor insurance fills the gap

Visitor to Canada insurance is private emergency medical coverage bought for the length of the stay. Depending on the plan, it typically covers emergency medical treatment, hospitalization, diagnostic tests, prescription drugs for the emergency, and repatriation home — the exact costs OHIP won't touch for a non-resident. Plans are available for tourists, visiting family members, new immigrants awaiting eligibility, and returning Canadians without active provincial coverage.

Coverage amounts commonly range from $50,000 to $150,000+, and premiums depend mostly on the visitor's age and health — the same drivers we break down in our Super Visa insurance cost guide.

The Super Visa special case

If your parent or grandparent is coming on a Super Visa, insurance isn't just a good idea — it's an IRCC requirement: at least $100,000 of emergency medical coverage from a Canadian insurer (or IRCC-approved provider), valid for 365 days, with proof required as part of the application. See our Super Visa insurance page for how the requirement works and what it costs.

Don't wait until arrival: visitor insurance is best purchased before the visitor lands in Canada. Buying after arrival is possible with many insurers but often comes with a waiting period before coverage starts — a few days when the visitor is completely exposed.

Frequently asked questions

Does OHIP cover visitors or tourists in Ontario?

No. OHIP only covers eligible Ontario residents. Visitors and tourists must pay for care out of pocket unless they carry private visitor insurance.

Is there still a 3-month OHIP waiting period for newcomers?

No — Ontario permanently eliminated it in 2024. Eligible newcomers are covered from their first day, but visitors still don't qualify at all.

What does a hospital stay cost without insurance?

An ER visit commonly costs $1,000+ before tests and treatment, and admissions run several thousand dollars per day. Serious emergencies reach tens of thousands.

How much coverage should a visitor buy?

Commonly $50,000–$150,000 depending on age, health and length of stay. Super Visa applicants must carry at least $100,000 for 365 days — that part isn't optional.

The bottom line

OHIP will never cover your visiting family members — and Ontario hospitals will bill them directly at rates that can devastate a family budget. The fix is simple and inexpensive relative to the risk: arrange visitor insurance before they arrive, sized to their age, health, and stay.
